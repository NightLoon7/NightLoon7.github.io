Neil Armstrong

First man on the moon
Neil Armstrong in July 1969

    Major Events from Neil Armstrongs life:

  • 1930 - Born on August 5th in Wapakoneta, Ohio
  • 1944 - Earned a student flight certificate on his sixteenth birthday, then soloed in August, all before he had a driver's license
  • 1947 - Begins studying aeronautical engineering at Purdue University under the Holloway Plan. Successful applicants committed to two years of study, followed by two years of flight training and one year of service in the U.S. Navy as an aviator, then completion of the final two years of their bachelor's degree
  • 1949 - Receives call-up from the navy on January 26th and by February 24th becomes a midshipman
  • 1950 - Makes his first aircraft carrier landing on March 2nd and is a fully qualified naval aviator by the end of August
  • 1951 - Called into action with the Navy to go fight in the Korean war. When that ended, he returned to college
  • 1952 - Begins working with the organization that would later be called NASA
  • 1956 - Marries a lady named Janet Shearon
  • 1962 - Moves his family to Texas so he could work with the astronaut program
  • 1966 - Neil and his flight mate, David Scott, were launched into space to dock with the Gemini Agena vehicle. The dock was successful, but the mission had to be cut short
  • 1969 - Neil Armstrong, along with two other astronauts, landed on the moon. Armstrong drove the Lunar Module. Armstrong and his flight mates received many wards for their moon mission, which included the Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Space Medal of Honor
  • 1971 - Leaves NASA to become a professor in Ohio, at the University of Cincinnati
  • 1986 - Helps investigate the failed space mission, the Challenger. The Challenger blew up after launch, killing its crew
  • 2006 - Liked his privacy and stayed out of the spotlight for many years. Gave a TV interview on 60 Minutes and his biography is released later in the year
  • 2010 - President Barack Obama makes cuts to the space program and Armstrong publicly speaks out against this decision
  • 2012 - Dies at the age of 82, after complications from heart surgery
